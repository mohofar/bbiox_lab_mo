Research Projects

Our research focuses on understanding skeletal development and disorders through advanced biomechanical analysis, computational modeling, and medical imaging. We combine experimental approaches with cutting-edge computational methods to advance treatment strategies.

Skeletal Shape Analysis and Disease Prediction

Developing advanced computational methods to analyze bone shape variations and their relationship to skeletal diseases. This project combines medical imaging with machine learning to identify early markers of skeletal disorders and improve diagnostic accuracy.

Medical Imaging Machine Learning Shape Analysis Clinical Applications
Collaboration with Department of Orthopedics, Erasmus MC

Biomechanical Analysis of Joint Development

Investigating the mechanical forces and their influence on joint development and adaptation. This research combines experimental biomechanics with computational modeling to understand how mechanical loading affects skeletal growth and remodeling.

Biomechanics Tissue Engineering Computational Modeling Joint Development
In collaboration with Faculty of Mechanical Engineering, TU Delft

Computer Vision for Skeletal Assessment

Developing advanced computer vision and deep learning techniques for automated analysis of medical images in skeletal assessment. This project aims to improve the accuracy and efficiency of diagnostic procedures in orthopedics.

Computer Vision Deep Learning Medical Imaging Diagnostic Tools
Joint project with Medical Imaging Department

Patient-Specific Implant Design

Creating innovative approaches for designing and optimizing patient-specific implants using computational modeling and additive manufacturing. This research focuses on improving implant performance and surgical outcomes through personalized solutions.

Implant Design FEM Analysis 3D Printing Surgical Planning
Collaboration with Clinical Partners
